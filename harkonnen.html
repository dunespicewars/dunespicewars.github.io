Faction Bonus
  • + Can use the Oppression Ability on Harkonnen villages
  • + Can use Corruption on Landsraad resolutions, causing a loss of Landsraad Standing for the elected faction
  • + +5% Village resource production per active militia
  • − -10% Village resource production

At 5k Hegemony
  • + Each day, you have 10% chance per Agent in Counterintelligence to convert captured Agents. This agent produces -10% of every resource but Infiltration
  • + Allows you to assign an agent to a mission to reduce its cost and preparation time. The agent will be sacrificed

At 10k Hegemony
  • + Give a chance to not consume an operation when launching it, according to its difficulty
  • + Grants +1 additional agent slot in each non-faction field

Councilors

Feyd-Rautha Harkonnen
Difficulty: Hard
+ While at least one ally Village is under Oppression: Gain +10% Influence production & +100% Agent recruitment speed

+ Gain 10 Influence upon killing a Rebel

"Glossu’s younger brother and the Baron’s favored nephew, Feyd-Rautha has all it takes to uphold his family’s sadistic culture. An unmatched duelist and charismatic leader, he is the chosen na-Baron of House Harkonnen, destined to take over his uncle’s legacy."

Piter De Vries
Difficulty: Hard
+ Ornithopters and Stealth Probes have Stealth and generate +2 Intel while in enemy territory

+ Each day, you have a 10% chance to convert capture Agents per Agent in Counterintelligence

"Piter is a twisted Mentat; that is a human computer, but bred on planet Tleilax with much less ethical constraints in mind that their regular counterparts."

Glossu Rabban
Difficulty: Easy
+ +1 Militia slot

− -20% Militia Power

"Nicknamed “the Beast” after murdering his own father, the Count of Lankiveil is every bit bit as evil and corrupt as his uncle, the Baron Harkonnen. Rabban only rules through violence and terror, making him a dreaded and despised leader."

Iakin Nefud
Difficulty: Easy
+ 60% of your Military Units costs is refunded upon their death

+ -60% Combat Drugs mission cost

"Iakin is the Captain of the House Harkonnen guard. While regarded as decently reliable while on duty, he is a known Semuta addict, which, to some, is enough to cast doubt on all of his abilities."

    Statecraft

    Cruel Reputation

    • + Targeting at least one enemy faction when launching an operation gives 20 Influence
    • + Using an operation in a Region gives it -20% Authority cost to annex a Village for 2 days
    • + Unlocks the Interrogation Center building
    It is a good think they are not too fond of us. It keeps them on their toes, and us on our feet

    Expansion

    Instill Fear

    • + Unlocks the Investment Office building
    • + -50% Authority needed to capture back a Village that used to belong to you
    • + Apply Instill Fear (x5 max) on pillaged villages:
      • + -5% Authority cost to annex a Village
    It is by my will alone I set my mind in motion

    Economy

    Martial Economy

    • + -30% military units Solari Upkeep
    • + -30% military units Solari cost
    • + You can assign 1 more Agents to CHOAM Infiltration
    Let them complain!

    Other Factions

    House Atreides

    The stellar reputation of House Atreides owes a lot to its leader’s commanding style: firm yet honorable and fair. By imperial decree, the Atreides have just inherited the stewardship of Arrakis and have a lot of work to do to take over from the Harkonnens.

    Smugglers

    There’s a fine line between smugglers and full-fledged bandits, and Esmar Tuek is adept at walking that line. Smugglers can provide anything to anyone, provided they put the price… and do not look at the specifics too closely.

    Fremen

    Fremen are the only people native to Arrakis and will do anything to protect their way against the offworlders. While their leader, Liet-Kynes, doesn’t originally come from Dune, she is every bit as determined and attached to the planet as the next Fremen.
